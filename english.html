To Everyone!

-Today - (May 17, 2015 -year) is the greatest -day in history. -Today, I made the greatest discovery in the history of Earth’s civilization! Perhaps this is too bold a statement, but only if there were unknown civilizations in Earth’s history that might have existed and vanished without a trace due to a catastrophe similar to the one we know of, which occurred 65 million years ago and caused the extinction of the dinosaurs. And if, during my lifetime, it becomes known that a being in such a civilization made a discovery like mine, I will offer my apologies to that being. Also, it’s possible that, independently of me, someone else could have made the same discovery at the same time but, due to modesty or some other objective reason, they delayed and I beat them to the official publication. In that case, I will also apologize and agree to share the glory and material rewards proportional to each of our contributions to the discovery. If determining the shares proves difficult, I’m willing to split all dividends equally. Now, I’d like to express my gratitude to everyone who contributed—or at least didn’t hinder— my scientific endeavors that led to this greatest discovery. First and foremost, my wife Nelya, my dog Rocky, and all the people close and distant to me. I especially want to highlight the significance of my grandson Emil’s actions, whose behavior led me to realize that humanity, throughout its entire conscious history, has been deeply mistaken about the fundamental phenomena and laws of nature. I’ll elaborate on this later. Casting aside false modesty and understanding the importance of my discovery, I declare: the Nobel Prize, which they will naturally want to award me, is too small to measure my contribution to science. Therefore, I preemptively refuse the title of Nobel laureate, though I won’t refuse its monetary component—although I believe I deserve all the funds currently available to the Nobel Committee and all the funds it might ever have in the future. To those who might envy my fame, I hasten to say that all the money I receive from the recognition of my contribution to science will be donated to help those affected by the implementation of my discovery—and such people will inevitably appear. I fully understand that my discovery will shake the scientific world of the planet and propel it toward new breakthroughs. As a result, my discovery will touch every inhabitant of Earth. All existing knowledge gained by humanity through local evolution must be revised and corrected. We will have to acknowledge the erroneous nature of the theories and ideas of such famous scientists as Einstein, Darwin, Newton, and all others. Even a seemingly hopeless field like time travel will receive a colossal boost in development, and time travel will become routine for anyone who wishes it. But first, we must admit that all knowledge and all sciences are wrong, and all textbooks need to be rewritten. To minimize costs at this initial stage of the scientific revolution, I suggest not burning the incorrect textbooks and scientific works but simply having everyone take a marker or ballpoint pen and correct the mistakes in them. It will be much harder to fix the vast amount of software for personal computers, industrial robots, and various control systems—even devices based on the simplest microcontrollers. In some cases, this correction may prove impossible if the source code is lost or forgotten by its authors, and sparse comments fail to reconstruct their thought processes. Such software will be irretrievably lost to humanity. A sad fate awaits all manufacturers of transportation—cars, all types of aircraft, all types of watercraft, bicycles, scooters, skateboards, and even skis. All these devices will need to be recalled for correction, which will inevitably bankrupt their producers. All existing brands and giant corporations will cease to exist—Microsoft, IBM, Sony, and all the others. Even seemingly harmless companies, like manufacturers of expensive Swiss watches, will vanish from the face of the Earth. For a while, Earth’s civilization will face complete collapse. During this transitional period, all vehicles will stop functioning correctly, and all military equipment will also cease to operate, rendering all wars and conflicts impossible and frozen. For some time, humanity will be completely disoriented, with movement in any direction becoming particularly difficult—even scratching behind your ear without a mirror will be impossible. Television and radio will stop working—and thus stop lying—cellular communication will fail, and SMS will only be sendable to oneself. First and foremost, for a very objective reason, navigation systems like GLONASS and GPS will fail, as all navigation and communication satellites will refuse to function in this new reality. All previous standards and measures will cease to work, necessitating the development of new standards, especially for time and space (length). As for all the clocks we currently use—wristwatches with hands, street clocks, city clocks, home alarm clocks, and others—they must be immediately reworked. The simplest solution I propose is to remove the hands and dial, install the dial on the back of the clock, and attach the hands to the back on an extended axis. As a result, the hands will move in reverse— counterclockwise—and finally show the correct time. Redesigning all digital clocks is much simpler and within the capability of any child over one year old, but I won’t digress into that now —I’ll explain later. Despite the grim prospects of this transitional period, humanity is mature enough to inevitably overcome the crisis. Some measures to mitigate the crisis must be implemented immediately— only then can we avoid the need to reverse the rotation of all celestial bodies: satellites, planets of the solar system, the Sun, all stars and galaxies, all universes, as well as smaller cosmic objects like comets and meteorites. Only these measures can prevent the total destruction of both the visible and invisible parts of the cosmos and delay the onset of a new BIG BANG, which, over thirteen billion years ago, created Space and Time and our universe. If these urgent measures are carried out successfully, the complete collapse of the world order can be avoided, industry can be restored, and wars and conflicts can be unfrozen. But the world will never be the same again. Whether we want it or not, it will be a new world where even cannons, rockets, and ordinary bullets will fly backward and strike the devices and people who fired them. Whether we want it or not, we will all have to relearn everything—just as, after birth, the world appeared upside-down to each of us due to the peculiarities of vision, we will now have to flip it back, and gradually everything will fall into place. But some unpopular measures will be unavoidable. We will have to call things by their true names. We will have to acknowledge that an electron is not a negatively charged particle, and a positron is not positively charged, and correctly assign their true polarities. We will have to teach children correctly that electric current is the movement of positively charged particles through a conductor from the positive electrode of a power source to the negative one. We will need to take colored markers and correct the polarity on all existing power sources, and use black and red paint to change the polarity markings on car batteries. For now, I warn car owners against swapping their battery terminals themselves, as this will lead to predictable results. It’s better left to manufacturers later on. It will also be necessary to reposition the magnetic poles of all permanent magnets and Earth’s poles, recognizing that we live in the Southern Hemisphere of the globe. We will also need to correctly establish the phase sequence of three-phase 50 Hz and 60 Hz electrical grids or abandon them entirely, considering that our understanding of the sinusoidal waveform was fundamentally wrong. Naively trusting oscilloscope screens with horizontal time sweeps, we thought sinusoidal current flowed through wires and the ether from left to right—now we know it’s the opposite, from right to left. All types of electric motors, it turns out, rotate backward and incorrectly, and all vehicles move us in the opposite direction of where we need to go. Correcting mistakes and dispelling humanity’s millennia-long misconceptions about the nature of Space and Time is an inevitable process. I don’t blame the scientists who drew incorrect conclusions and created flawed theories and laws as a result. Neither Einstein, nor Darwin, nor Newton, nor anyone else is at fault—they were simply too trusting. My only grievance is with the so-called great mathematicians; they could have calculated the error before anyone else but either didn’t want to or were too afraid to take responsibility for the consequences. I, however, am not afraid and am willing to answer to the judgment of history. I believe the sooner we correct this mistake, the sooner an era of universal prosperity and progress will dawn on Earth, and the long-awaited communism will finally arrive, where everyone can do nothing if they choose and still receive everything life has to offer without limits. As a result, migration and wars will disappear, and all surviving refugees and displaced persons will return to their homes. The sooner humanity breaks free from the confines of the solar system and reaches alien civilizations—which, as it turns out, have not yet made a discovery like mine. If they had, Earth would already have been conquered by them or, at best, part of an interplanetary galactic alliance. It’s now clear what nonsense those so-called astronomers have been feeding us. It’s clear that black holes are not holes at all but rather solid objects of small size with enormous mass. They claim our universe, after the Big Bang, is made of matter and is continuously expanding, with all interstellar space filled with dark energy. Now it’s evident that it’s the opposite—our entire universe is made of antimatter and continuously contracts for each of us, shrinking to zero by the end of our lives. I’ll briefly recount the incredible confluence of circumstances that created the necessary conditions and pushed me toward this genius discovery. Truthfully, I have no answer to why it was me and not someone else—why providence chose me, and why now. Of course, I’m a special person, but even for me, such a discovery is overwhelming. The more I think about it, the more I’m convinced that such a discovery was destined for me and me alone. An event like this, a discovery of this magnitude, happens no more than once in the lifetime of any universe. Despite there being an infinite number of universes like ours, made of antimatter and matter, constantly colliding, contracting, and exploding, a discovery like mine, on the scale of a universe’s life—and even more so Earth’s civilization—is extraordinarily rare, and I’m proud to have achieved it. Reflecting on my journey, I conclude that several events made this discovery possible. The first happened (my apologies to historians who will undoubtedly be interested in my biography for the approximate dates) when my eldest son was about five or six years old. Lacking other tools, he took a hammer and tried to do to his grandmother’s clock what I’ve already advised you to do with analog clocks. He didn’t succeed back then because, at his age, he could only handle redesigning digital clocks—but those were rare at the time, and no one let him near them. The second significant event involved digital clocks and featured my grandson, the son of my younger son (whom I mentioned earlier). He categorically refused to write a program for digital clocks on a PIC microcontroller. His protest against our flawed understanding of time’s essence sent a powerful impulse into my subconscious. My granddaughter Ira played a different role—she didn’t protest when I suggested we make a birthday gift for her mom in the form of digital clocks. We successfully made the gift, and that event imprinted an impulse of opposite polarity in my subconscious, which only delayed the historic moment of my discovery. Fortunately, another event brought that date closer: I was hired into one of the departments of our city’s administration, specifically the communal services division. While my colleagues drove massive garbage trucks to clean out trash bins and an army of janitors swept the streets daily, I was entrusted with the no less critical task of overseeing the city’s clocks—five in total—and ensuring their uninterrupted and accurate operation. These old clocks, which, when turned off, showed the exact time twice a day across all twenty-four Earth time zones, influenced my subconscious with yet another powerful impulse through their unsynchronized operation and refusal to show accurate time when switched on, demonstrating that our notions of time were wrong. And so, today—May 17, 2015—this historic day, after lunch, as I lay on my couch in a half- dozing state, all the impulse energy accumulated in my subconscious burst forth with tremendous force, and it hit me—I unraveled the mystery of humanity’s misconceptions. Everything instantly fell into place; a complete fusion of Space, Time, and my subconscious occurred. And it pained me that only now, and only I, with a delay of four billion years in Earth’s history and over thirteen billion years since our universe’s birth, could grasp the meaning of Time and its influence on Space. If this discovery had been made earlier, if someone else had made it long before I was born, I’d be far happier—living in a brilliant world, not in a poor, ravaged, partially occupied country, a world with no place for scoundrels and “fathers of nations” like Hitler, Stalin, Putin, and other filth. Now, to the most important part—the essence of my discovery: what I realized lying on the couch. It’s no coincidence that I’ve described my posture and this now-historic piece of furniture in such detail. I understand that many sculptors and artists will want to immortalize this piece of furniture and me in history, cast it in granite—so this information is for them. So, what is the essence of my discovery? Behind the apparent simplicity of the answer lies the thirteen-billion- year history of our universe, a long journey that, through the labor of many generations, countless misconceptions, and mistakes, led to an understanding of Space and Time. The core postulate of my discovery, capable of turning the world upside down and inside out, is this assertion: time cannot be a positive quantity. Time has a minus sign. Time is exclusively a negative quantity. It always decreases; it never adds to anyone or anything. Every -moment, every -second, -minute, -hour, -day, -week, -month, -year—is time taken away, lost, a spent life. Now it’s clear why the title of my message “To Everyone” and all temporal parameters bear a minus sign. Now it’s understandable why even an infant can fix digital clocks—all they need is a marker of the right color to place a bold -minus sign before the highest digit. Now it’s evident why Space, like Time, is a negative quantity. According to the well-known and undeniably correct formula, the distance traveled by any object in uniform motion equals velocity multiplied by time. Since time is a negative quantity, the distance traveled by an object is also negative. Though I’m no expert in mechanics—or any science—I assert with 100% certainty that the result of distance traveled in non-uniform motion will also be negative. What does this mean? It means that, due to erroneous notions of Space and Time, humanity hasn’t just been stagnant in its development—it’s been racing backward at breakneck speed, in real, virtual, and moral terms. This explains much of Earth’s history: conquest wars, cruelty, lies, betrayal, theft, the degradation of family values, same-sex marriages, the loss of concepts like nobility. As a dilettante in all sciences, I don’t claim to provide a precise analysis of the crisis caused by my discovery, and, of course, escaping it won’t be simple. Simply placing minus signs in physics textbooks with a ballpoint pen won’t save Earth’s civilization. Only coordinated efforts across all of Earth’s Space and Time will help us emerge from this crisis. P.S. I believe that, within two years, Earth’s economy will recover from the recession because, by then, through our collective efforts, we’ll have corrected all the mistakes humanity has made over millennia and mitigated their consequences.
